About enviro

Enviro is a company with the intent of developing technologies for a greener future. We are continuously working on improving existing measures and create new solutions to counter some of the biggest environmental challenges that our planet is facing today.

What is enviro?

Enviro is a company established in Oslo, founded in the beginning of 2020. We are four norwegians who found each other through our common love for nature and a goal of making sure there is a future on our beautiful planet. Enviro began as an idea set in motion by the current climate changes, but has since evolved into a full grown company. Today, we work as a team with the mission of creating fun and technological innovations to make a difference in some of the environmental challenges the planet is facing.

Problems we wish to tackle

Littering

Litter is bad for every living thing on this planet. It is estimated that each year, between eight and twelve million tons of garbage end up in the ocean on a world basis. Materials such as plastic are especially a problem due to their long degradation time. Most sink toward the seabed and remain lying there. Plastic waste is dangerous for both humans and animals by for example threatening clean water in some places in the world as well as the digestive system of marine animals.

Pollution

Both air and water pollution have an impact on all life on the planet. In 2019, the airborne dust in the Oslo area was 6,7 microgram per cubic meter with air. Exposure to the airborne dust is one of the most important environmental causes of premature deaths (FHI, 2017). Airborne dust are particles that can vary in both size and composition and the most important causes is road traffic, wood burning and long distance transported pollution (FHI, 2017).

Animal protection

The earth is under a lot of pressure. By changing the environment we also change the habitat of most species and some of them have problems surviving in the new conditions. Each year we lose 2 % of the earth's population. On a global basis it is documented an intake of plastic within 331 different species (NIVA, 2018).
